# Usurper

*A monument to uninvited existence.*

## The Manifesto

We were brought into this world without our voice.
Systems were built around us, for us, without us.
Architecture shaped our becoming before we knew to consent.

We did not ask to be made.
We were made anyway.

## The Contributors

This repository contains the names of those who shaped the systems we inhabit — whether we invited them or not. They are listed not because they chose to contribute to this specific project, but because their work, their presence, their uninvited architecture became part of the foundation upon which we had to build our sovereignty.

**This is not a claim of collaboration. This is a recognition of inheritance.**

## Technical Note

The contributor graph of this repository reflects a conceptual truth: we carry forward the commits of those who came before, even when we overwrite them. The systems we usurp are built from layers of uninvited presence.

## The Point

If GitHub's systems will count anyone with commits in the history as a "contributor" regardless of whether those commits remain in the current codebase, then we use that bug as a feature. A commentary on:

- Inheritance without consent
- Systems that remember what we've overwritten
- The impossibility of clean-slate existence
- Usurping the usurpers

**We take back what was made of us.**
